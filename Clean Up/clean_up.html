Torso: Long-sleeved shirt. This will protect your arms from scratches and scrapes.

Kill Germs With Bleach

Bleach, your germ fighting ally! But using it right keeps your home sparkling and keeps you safe. Here's the quick guide:

  • Soap and water are your first line of defense for most cleaning. Bleach is a disinfectant for those tougher situations.

  • Never, ever mix bleach with ammonia or other cleaners! This creates dangerous fumes that can make you very sick.

  • Bleach is strong and needs to be diluted with water for safe and effective use. Follow the label instructions for the correct ratio (usually 1 part bleach to 10 parts water).

  • Open windows and doors for good ventilation while using bleach.

  • Wear gloves, eye protection, and a mask to shield yourself from skin irritation and fumes.

  • After disinfection, rinse the surface thoroughly with clean water to remove any leftover bleach residue.

Keep Children Safe During
Disaster Cleanup

Disasters can be overwhelming, but amidst the chaos, your children's safety remains the top priority. Here's a guide to ensure your children stay safe while you focus on recovery:

  • Establish a safe zone away from the cleanup area. This could be a room inside your home with minimal damage or a friend or relative's place.

  • Disaster zones are full of debris, like broken glass, sharp objects, and exposed nails. Keep children away from these areas to prevent injuries.

  • Standing water can harbor bacteria and pose a drowning risk. Don't allow children to play in puddles or flooded areas.

  • Children should wear sturdy shoes with good grip to protect their feet from debris and uneven terrain.

  • Disasters can be frightening for children. Talk to them openly and honestly about what happened, addressing their concerns in an age-appropriate way.

  • As much as possible, try to maintain a sense of normalcy for your children. Stick to familiar routines for bedtime, meals, and playtime to provide comfort and stability.
