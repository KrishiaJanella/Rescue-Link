Landslide

A landslide is a geological event characterized by the movement of a mass of rock, earth, or debris down a slope. Landslides can vary greatly in size and speed, ranging from slow, gradual movements to rapid and catastrophic events. They can be triggered by various factors such as heavy rainfall, earthquakes, volcanic activity, human activities like construction or mining, or natural processes like erosion.
