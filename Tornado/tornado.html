Tornado

While tornadoes are uncontrollable natural phenomena, adherence to proper safety protocols can significantly mitigate the risks to human health during and after these events. These protocols are crucial as tornadoes generate extremely high winds and propel debris, leading to potential injuries. Furthermore, the aftermath of a tornado can introduce additional hazards.

Prepare for a tornado

When a tornado warning sounds or you see a tornado approaching, take immediate action to protect yourself and others. Find shelter quickly in your designated safe place to avoid injury from flying debris and the powerful winds of a tornado.

Learn More

Response during a tornado

Hearing a Tornado or seeing one around you, prioritize your safety and the safety of those around you by swiftly seeking refuge in your safe space. This immediate response is vital to minimize the risk of injury from flying debris and the wind forces of a tornado.

Learn More

Recover after a tornado

Following the devastation of a tornado, focus on ensuring the well-being of yourself and loved ones, then begin securing your property and seeking assistance for cleanup and repairs.
